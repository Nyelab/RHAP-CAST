RIVER HERRING AVOIDANCE PROGRAM FORECAST

The River Herring Avoidance Program Forecast (RHAPCAST) forecasts regions to maximize encounters of target species (Atlantic herring or Atlantic mackerel) while minimizing river herring bycatch. Forecasts are provided about one week in advance to help you plan. Look for a new forecast each Monday! Generally, the warmer colors on the maps (red/brown) represent areas that are predicted to have a low encounter probability of the species, and the cooler colors (blue) represent areas that are predicted to have high encounters of the species.

December 13 – 19 Forecast to maximize Atlantic herring encounters while minimizing river herring encounters

This map shows areas that predict a high encountering probability of Atlantic herring (blue) and areas that either also have a high encountering probability of river herring or low encountering probability of Atlantic herring (red/brown). The joint probability likelihood was estimated for each grid cell by multiplying the encounter probability of Atlantic herring by one subtracted by the encounter probability of river herring. The bright red area is a restricted fishing area for midwater trawls, and the solid black lines represent the river herring and shad bycatch cap management areas.
